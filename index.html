2. Circulation Effects Study

In marine hydrodynamics, circulation Γ is the governing mechanism behind hydrofoil lift generation. By the Kutta–Joukowski theorem, the hydrodynamic lift per unit span is L′ = ρ U∞ Γ, where ρ = 1025 kg/m³ for seawater. The chart below plots lift coefficient CL, scaled circulation Γ, and surface velocity ratio Vt/U∞ as functions of angle of attack, revealing the linear attached-flow regime and theoretical stall onset around α ≈ 14°–16°.

Kutta–Joukowski Theorem

The lift per unit span on a body in potential flow equals the product of fluid density, freestream velocity, and the total circulation around the body:

L′ = ρ · U∞ · Γ

For a thin symmetric aerofoil the circulation grows linearly with angle of attack in the attached-flow regime (α ≲ 15°), giving a lift-curve slope of 2π per radian.

Vortex Sheet Model

The foil surface is replaced by N panels each carrying a vortex of strength γ (m/s). The no-penetration condition at each panel midpoint yields a linear system:

Σ Aᵢⱼ γⱼ = −V∞·n̂ᵢ

The Kutta condition (γ_TE = 0) is enforced as an additional equation. Total circulation Γ = Σ γⱼ Δsⱼ, from which CL = 2Γ / (U∞ · c).

Pressure Distribution

Local surface pressure is found from Bernoulli's equation. The pressure coefficient is:

Cp = 1 − (Vt / U∞)²

Suction peaks appear near the leading edge on the upper surface; higher α sharpens the suction peak and moves it further forward, eventually causing separation.

Stagnation Points

Two stagnation points exist where |V| → 0. At α = 0° both sit symmetrically at the leading edge and trailing edge. As α increases:

x_S₁ ≈ 0.5(1 − cos α · 0.9)

S₁ migrates to the pressure (lower) surface. S₂ stays near the trailing edge by the Kutta condition, which enforces smooth flow departure there.
